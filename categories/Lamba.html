Lamba

Lamb is a tender and flavorful type of red meat that comes from young sheep, prized in cuisines worldwide for its rich taste and versatility. It is commonly used in dishes such as roasts, stews, curries, and grilled specialties, often seasoned with herbs like rosemary, mint, and thyme to enhance its natural flavor. Lamb is a good source of high-quality protein, iron, zinc, and vitamin B12, making it a nutritious option when enjoyed in moderation. Popular in Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, Indian, and European cooking, lamb dishes are often associated with festive occasions and family gatherings, bringing warmth and tradition to the table.
